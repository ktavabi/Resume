\begin{cvletter}
  I am writing to express my interest in the opening for a Data Scientist position in the Alzheimer's Disease Data Initiative (ADDI) at Gates Ventures.

  I'm drawn to this opportunity for several reasons. Firstly, the opportunity to continue doing reliable and impactful neuroscience research. I have a proven track record of success in academic neuroscience, most recently in my current job as a Research Science Engineer at the Institute for Learning and Brain Sciences (ILABS). A highlight from my time here was being promoted to University scientific staff (RSE/IV) after driving funding efforts and project planning for a series of neuroimaging studies. In one study, I developed a multimodal neuroimaging exam to characterize the neural correlates of language impairments in adolescents with autism spectrum disorders. For this project alone, I forged a collaboration between ILABS and the University of Washington Autism Center; and I also oversaw data acquisition, integration with the existing patient health database, analysis, and progress reporting. Second, I am enthusiastic about the open-source philosophy of fostering an inclusive and transparent ecosystem for scientific research. I also contributed to open-source Python software applications developed to analyze human electrophysiology. I employed reproducible workflows for data processing, wrangling and feature engineering, and statistical modeling. Third, an opportunity to continue cultivating creativity in translational research by developing general-purpose tools for data science, e.g., MLops. I applied novel Machine Learning algorithms (logit, Random forest) to analyze this dataset to combine or harmonized magnetic resonance imaging, spectroscopy, and dense-array timeseries sensor data for 35+ case-control participants. 
  
  Lastly, I am attracted to this role because of the opportunity to do translational neuroscience research in Alzheimer's by leveraging modern data science techniques like machine learning. According to my research on your company, It looks to me that the ADDI team and advisory board is a vibrant workforce with extensive domain expertise in Alzheimer's disorders. As a post-doc in the Department of Radiology at the Children's Hospital in Philadelphia, I designed a neuroimaging exam to `localize' language function in the brain to study language impairments in children with autism spectrum disorders. By combining psycholinguistics with electrophysiology, the resulting exam was fast, accurate, and reliable enough to be included in the department's battery of pre-operative brain mapping routines for the surgical treatment of epilepsy in pediatric patients. For outstanding translational research in cognitive impairments in children with autism spectrum disorders, I was awarded a college loan repayment scholarship by the NIH. However, I am most proud of being able to make an immediate and tangible impact on patient health with my research and the resulting functional localizer exam. A high level of organization and attention to detail is second nature to me, and I'm eager to apply my skills to tackle the challenges with data harmonization \& collaborative research to develop the ADDI Alzheimer's Disease Workbench.

  Thank you for your time and consideration. My previous experience makes me an ideal candidate for this position, and I hope to show the team firsthand what an asset I can be. I look forward to hearing back from you.

\end{cvletter}
